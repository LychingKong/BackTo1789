Emmanuel-Joseph Sieyès (1748 - 1836)

Emmanuel-Joseph Sieyès, commonly known as Abbé Sieyès, was a French clergyman and political writer, who became a leading voice in the Third Estate during the French Revolution. He played instrumental roles in both the opening and closing events of the Revolution and sought a government that reflected the Third Estate, which he believed was the true nation.
