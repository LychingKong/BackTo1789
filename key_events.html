The Women’s March on Versailles

(October 5–6, 1789)

→ Frustrated by food shortages and high bread prices, Parisian women marched from Paris to the Palace of Versailles.

→ The march forced King Louis XVI and his family to return to Paris.

→ This event symbolizes the shift of power from the monarchy to the revolutionaries and the beginning of the monarchy's decline.

The Abolition of the Monarchy

(September 21st, 1792)

→ The National Convention that was elected after the fall of the monarchy, declared France a republic and abolished the monarchy.

→ King Louis XVI was deposed and imprisoned, and the French monarchy was officially ended, setting the stage for the establishment of the First French Republic.

The Reign of Terror

(September 1793 - July 1794)

→ A period during the French Revolution led by Maximilien Robespierre.

→ Used extreme measures, including mass executions by guillotine and imprisonment, to suppress opposition.

→ Ended with Robespierre’s execution in July 1794, signaling a shift in the revolution’s direction.

The Estates General

→ An assembly comprising of the clergy, nobility and the peasants.

→ Was convened by king Louis XVI so that the three estates could air their grievance and advice to the king.

→ Failed because the first and second estates held more influence than the third.

The End of French Revolution

(The rise of Napoleon Era)

→ France's revolutionary armies, led by Napoleon and other generals, achieved significant victories against foreign coalitions, boosting Napoleon’s popularity as a military hero

→ He overthrew the Directory (1795–1799) and established the Consulate with the support of key political and military allies.

→ This marked the transition from revolutionary chaos to authoritarian rule.

Storming of Bastille

(July 14th, 1789)

→ The third estate, which are commoners and lower clergy, want to take control over the bastille that is a symbol of monarchy power.

→ Successfully seized control of Bastille and become French national holiday until now.

The Declaration of the rights of Man and Citizens

→ Adopted the declaration as the new foundation of a new constitutional framework on August 26,1789.

→ Inspired by the American enlightenment.

→ Promote equality, freedom, fair taxes and governance which seem to limited king authority.
